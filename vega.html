About the Dataset

This dataset contains video game sales records, with each row representing a single game release. Sales are reported in millions of units sold and include regional breakdowns (North America, Europe, Japan, and Other regions) and total global sales.

Variable Types

Variable Data Type Description
Name Nomianl Title of the video game
Platform Nominal Console or system
Year Ordinal Year of release
Genre Nominal Game genre classification
Publisher Nominal Publishing company
NA_Sales Numerical North America sales
EU_Sales Numerical Europe sales
JP_Sales Numerical Japan sales
Other_Sales Numerical Other region sales
Global_Sales Numerical Total worldwide sales

Key Insights

Global Sales Distribution

The distribution of global sales is strongly skewed. The mean global sales is y 0.5 million units. Whereas the median is significantly lower at around 0.17 million units. This suggets that most games sell on the lower end, but a few blockbuster titles achieve exceptionally high sales which skews the mean.

The large gap between mean, median and a high standard deviation highlights how in the video game industry, a limited number of titles generate a disporprtionate share of total revenue.

Regional Patterns

North America represents the largest aggregate sales market, followed closely by Europe. Despite "Action" video games being the most popular genre globally, Japan displays a greater prefernce for "Role-Playing" games. This aligns with certain expectations given the popularity of JRPGS, such as the Final Fantasy series both regionally and globally.
